# Quant Audit — Weather Prediction-Market Bot
**Date:** 2026-06-29 · **Auditor:** Claude (lead) + 14-agent investigation/verification workflow
**Scope:** all 8 layers (edge, data, backtest, strategy/code, storage, execution, risk, results) with active testing
**Method:** read the real code/data, reproduced the backtest, recomputed every headline metric from a copy of
`tradingbot.db`, ran adversarial verification (skeptics tasked to *refute* the load-bearing claims).
All numbers below were measured, not taken from comments/CLAUDE.md. Reproduction commands are in the last section.

> **Framing:** profitable weather trading is achievable. Everything here is written as *gap → fix*. The bot is
> closer than the live P&L suggests — it has a real, narrow seam of forecast skill (inland) that the current
> configuration is actively throwing away. But as deployed today it has **no demonstrated edge and is losing to
> the market**, and the belief that it is "at parity" is a backtest artifact. Fixing that is the whole job.

## 0. Bottom line (read this if nothing else)

1. **As deployed, the bot is the *worse* forecaster and is losing.** On the active 6-city book it is **−$924 over
   27 settled trades** (win rate 48%). The "+$211 / $10,211" headline you may have seen is **all cut-city tails and
   variance** (London n=3 +$825, Miami n=8 +$514) — the cities the bot actually trades are deeply negative.
2. **The root cause is mechanical and now understood.** The bot's day-ahead probability distribution is **3–4×
   flatter than the market's** (effective σ ≈ 5°F vs the market's ≈1.7°F). A flatter distribution *under-prices the
   market's most-likely bucket*, so the bot sees a fake **+30–45% "edge"** and bets **NO against the bucket the
   market correctly favours**. **42 of 44 settled trades are this exact "NO-on-the-market's-modal-bucket" trade.**
   It bets most confidently exactly where it is most wrong.
3. **The "we're at parity, judge on Brier" conclusion is a data-snooping artifact.** The calibration backtest reports
   the model at an *in-sample best-fit* width (1.5°F) the live bot never uses. **At the true live width (~5°F) the
   model is ~26% *worse* than the market** on the buckets it trades (Brier 0.22 vs 0.175 — independently reproduced).
   The backtest is further flattered by look-ahead (analysis-grade forecast centers) and an Asia timezone leak.
4. **There is no strategy P&L backtest anywhere.** Every "backtest" file is a *calibration/Brier* check. The actual
   trading rules (8% edge gate → Kelly → order-book fills → fees → settlement) have **never been simulated on
   history.** So "does the strategy make money" has literally never been tested off-line.
5. **But there is a real seam.** The model's forecast *center* genuinely **beats the market on Chicago** (and ties
   inland) at a narrow honest width — confirmed out-of-sample, CI excludes zero. The skill is real; the **over-wide
   σ (inflation 2.04) is destroying it,** and the bad coastal cities (Tokyo, HK) drown it. The path to profit is:
   **calibrate the width per-city, cut the structurally-bad coastal centers, redefine "edge," and prove it with a
   real cost-inclusive backtest before scaling.**

**The single most important open question** (the one genuine disagreement between investigators): *once the width
is calibrated and coastal cities cut, is the residual inland day-ahead edge actually positive after the 8% gate and
spread, or merely break-even?* This is unresolved and **only a real P&L backtest can answer it.** Do not scale a
dollar until it does.

## 2. The mechanism, in detail (findings #1, #2, #5 — the core)

This is the heart of the audit; everything else is downstream.

**What the bot believes:** "Our ensemble prices temperature buckets better than the market; when our probability
differs from the price by >8%, the market is mispriced and we bet."

**What is actually happening (measured):**
- The market resolves next-day temperature to an **implied σ ≈ 0.61°C / 1.70°F** (mean probability it places on the
  *actual winning bucket* = 0.585 for °C cities / 0.444 for °F cities, from 3,502 historical buckets).
- The live blend prices day-ahead at an **effective σ ≈ 2.3–2.6°C / ~5°F** (raw blend std ≈1.3°C × 2.04 inflation +
  2°F floor + 0.7/lead). That is **3–4× wider than the market.**
- A distribution that wide spreads its mass thin, so on the bucket the **market** thinks is most likely (≈50%), the
  **model** only places ≈13–27%. The model therefore computes a huge YES *under*-price → a **+30–45% edge to bet NO**.
- **42 of 44 settled trades (27/27 on the active book) are this single trade: "buy NO on the market's modal bucket."**
  Stated NO-side confidence 0.80–0.815; realized win rate **0.545** (active **0.481**). That +0.25 gap is the
  overconfidence, and it is **concentrated in exactly the mid-confidence region the bot trades** (the far-tail
  high-confidence buckets are well-calibrated — so the model isn't uniformly broken, it's broken where it bets).

**Why this is the worst possible failure mode:** the 8% edge gate *ranks and selects trades by the size of the
disagreement* — i.e. it deliberately seeks out the model's largest deviations from a calibrated market, which are
the model's **largest errors**. It's a machine for harvesting your own forecast mistakes. The adversarial check
confirms the signature: edge magnitude is **negatively** related to winning (negative in all 9 leave-one-city-out
cuts; tertile win-rates 0.71 → 0.47 → 0.47), and the overconfidence gap *widens* with edge size (+0.22 low-edge vs
+0.30 high-edge). *(Honest caveat: at n=44 this negative correlation is not statistically significant — CI crosses
zero. The durable, load-bearing claim is the **absence of any positive selectivity**: the upper bound on a positive
edge-vs-win correlation is only +0.05. The "edge" provides no protection and the biggest ones are the biggest
mirages.)*

**The deeper truth the σ machinery is telling you:** σ-inflation 2.04 was *honestly* fit so the ensemble spread
matches the forecast's RMSE-vs-station (~2°C). That RMSE includes **grid-cell-vs-point-station representativeness
error** that the market does **not** carry (the market reads the actual station). So an honestly-calibrated forecast
is structurally wider than the market, and **day-ahead we have no general forecast edge.** That is not fatalism —
it's the precise diagnosis that tells you where edge *can* come from (below).

**Fixes (in order of leverage):**
1. **Use the market as a Bayesian prior, not an adversary.** Blend `P_final = w·P_model + (1−w)·P_market` (small `w`
   day-ahead) *before* computing edge, and **refuse NO bets against the market's modal bucket** unless the corrected
   forecast center excludes that bucket by >~1.5σ. This deletes the 42 fake NO-on-modal edges outright — the single
   biggest P&L improvement available.
2. **Calibrate the probability** with isotonic/Platt scaling fit on the reliability curve (you have 3,502 labelled
   buckets) so stated conviction ≈ realized frequency *before* the gate and Kelly ever see it.
3. **Redefine edge as a falsifiable, shape-based mechanism:** pre-register "*on events where our center agrees with
   the market's implied center (<0.5°F), our full bucket distribution is better calibrated than the market's*," and
   only trade where that is measured to be true. Disagreeing with the market on the *level* is noise (the
   market-gap guardrail already concedes the market nails the level); any real edge must be in distribution *shape*
   around a *shared* center.

## 3. Why you couldn't see it — the backtest is not trustworthy (findings #3, #4, #6)

The calibration backtest (`backend/data/calibration_backfill.py`) is the instrument that produced the "≈ parity,
judge on Brier" conclusion. It has three flattering errors and one fundamental gap:

**(a) In-sample σ snooping [reproduced ✅].** It sweeps σ over the *same* events it scores, picks the minimum-Brier
σ (1.5°F), and reports the model there. The live bot does not price at 1.5°F. Re-scoring the identical records at
the real live width:

| Set (contested buckets the bot trades) | Market Brier | Model @ 1.5°F (snooped) | Model @ 2.7°F (its own "live" label) | Model @ **~5.0°F (true live)** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| CONTESTED, n=725 | **0.175** | 0.175 ("parity") | 0.192 | **0.220 (≈26% worse)** |
| Whole active set | 0.148 | — | — | **0.191** |

The model loses *monotonically* as width grows from the snooped 1.5°F to the deployed 5°F. **The "parity" rests
entirely on a width the bot never uses.** (The harness's own annotated "live floor+lead = 2.7°F" is itself wrong —
it assumes the 2°F floor binds, but raw×2.04 ≈ 4.3°F dominates it.)

**(b) Asia timezone leak in the market proxy.** The "fair day-ahead" market price is snapshotted at **06:00 UTC** on
the target date. For US cities that's ~01:00 local (genuinely pre-high, fair). For **Tokyo/Hong Kong it's 14–15:00
local — *after* the daily high has formed**, so that "day-ahead" price already knows the answer. Measured: Asian
market Brier 0.166 at the leaked snapshot vs **0.285 at a truly pre-high snapshot (+72% worse)**. This makes the
market look artificially unbeatable for exactly the cities the bot loses worst on, and the leaked price even selects
which buckets count as "contested." Fix: snapshot at a true pre-day, lead-aligned time (e.g. prior-day 18:00 UTC).

**(c) Forecast look-ahead.** The reconstructed "day-ahead" center comes from `historical-forecast-api`, which serves
the *freshest available run* (short-lead/analysis-grade), so it's **more accurate than the ~24h-lead forecast the
bot actually makes.** Tell-tale: reconstructed center MAE is ~1.4°F for the °C cities (analysis-grade) vs a
realistic day-ahead ~2.5–3.5°F, and the in-sample optimal σ (1.35°F) is impossibly tight for a real day-ahead
forecast. **So the model's real-time skill is *worse* than even this backtest shows.** Fix: reconstruct from the
lead-correct ensemble run.

**(d) No strategy P&L backtest at all [confirmed ✅].** The harness computes Brier/reliability/ECE only. It never
applies the 8% net-edge gate, the market-gap guardrail, Kelly sizing, the order-book VWAP walk, fees, maker/taker
routing, or settlement P&L. **"Does the strategy beat the price net of fees" has never been simulated.** This is the
highest-value thing to *build*, because a trustworthy backtest is what lets you scale a real edge with confidence.

> **A proper event-driven P&L backtest needs:** (1) point-in-time forecast distribution at *scan time* from a
> lead-correct ensemble run; (2) the *contemporaneous* CLOB order book at scan time (fix the leak in (b) first);
> (3) the real `passes_threshold` gates; (4) `calculate_kelly_size` off a simulated bankroll; (5) `walk_asks_for_cash`
> VWAP fills + fee; (6) settle on the known winner with `calculate_pnl`; (7) aggregate P&L, hit-rate, drawdown,
> P&L-net-of-cost. **Available:** resolved outcomes (Gamma), historical YES prices (CLOB prices-history).
> **Hard/missing:** historical *full order-book depth* at scan time (prices-history gives only top/mid → fills must
> be approximated conservatively) and a lead-resolved archived ensemble. Build this before any go-live decision.

## 4. The seam of real skill (finding #6 — the constructive core)

The adversarial skeptic tasked with refuting "no slice beats the market" instead **found the exception**, and it's
the most important positive result in the audit:

- At the live width (~5°F) **no active city beats the market** — every CI ≤ 0; whole-set market−model = −0.043,
  CI[−0.056,−0.030]. The HK/London P&L "wins" are **refuted as skill** (at the live width they are the *worst*
  forecasting slices; the wins are variance + the Asia leak).
- **BUT Chicago's forecast *center* genuinely beats the market** at a narrow honest width (~1.25–1.5°F):
  model Brier 0.119 vs market 0.146, **95% CI [+0.005, +0.046] excludes zero**, robust across 3 contested bands and
  **3 out-of-sample time splits** (train-best width lands at ~1.25°F every time). Chicago beats the market for all
  widths ≤ 3.25°F. The inland/EU block (Chicago+NYC+Denver+Paris, no Asia leak) **ties** the market at 1.5°F.
- **The deficit is a width/calibration over-correction, not missing skill.** The inland center has real information;
  the global σ-inflation of 2.04 (→~5°F) is smearing it away. **Replacing the single global inflation with per-city
  day-ahead width calibration is the highest-upside single change in the bot.**

**Two hard limits on this good news (don't over-read it):**
1. **"After costs" is *not* demonstrated.** Chicago's +0.026 Brier edge is modest, it's measured on look-ahead-good
   centers, and there is no P&L backtest — so even Chicago is **unproven profitable after the 8% gate + spread.**
2. **The fix does *not* rescue Asia.** Tokyo (un-bias-corrected, coastal) and Hong Kong have genuinely bad centers
   and lose at *every* width → **cut them, don't re-widen them.** (Tokyo is −$951 live for a structural reason, not
   noise.)

## 5. Strategy, sizing & risk (findings #7, #8, #10, #11, #19, #21)

- **#7 Market-gap guardrail is too loose to do its job.** Its tolerance is ~one full bucket width, so it cannot veto
  the *single-bucket* center disagreements that generate the 27–78% "edges." This is why a "Paris high ≤24°C: model
  27% vs market 1%, +25.9% edge" survives to execution. **Fix:** tighten the gap tolerance to a fraction of bucket
  width and gate on *center agreement* (you already compute `event_market_mean`).
- **#8 Kelly is sized on the raw, overconfident probability** (~16× true Kelly in places); only the 2.5% per-trade
  cap prevents a blow-up, which turns Kelly into a *flat max bet on every confident call* — destroying the
  edge/confidence differentiation Kelly is supposed to provide. The `edge` argument to `calculate_kelly_size` is
  **dead code** (never used). **Fix:** size on the *calibrated* probability; until calibrated, **do not** bet bigger
  on bigger disagreement — that convexity points exactly the wrong way right now.
- **#10 No trailing-drawdown stop.** The realized equity curve slid near-monotonically from +$256 to −$1,008
  (max drawdown **−$1,264 / −12.6%**) and **no breaker ever fired**, because the only circuit-breaker is a single
  UTC-day realized-loss limit. The empirical loss mode is a *slow bleed*, not a catastrophic day. **Fix:** add a
  peak-to-trough / rolling-N-day equity halt (pause at ~8–10% off peak, manual re-enable).
- **#11 Daily breaker is ~7× too loose and lagged.** −$1,500 limit vs a $210 daily σ / −$535 worst day; and it's
  realized-only at UTC-midnight, ignoring open mark-to-market. **Fix:** tighten to ~5% and make it forward-looking
  by including unrealized MTM (the dashboard already computes live side prices).
- **#19/#21 Correlation & odds.** The 7% city-day cap correctly bounds single-event loss, but a *systematic*
  regime bias (e.g. GFS cold-on-highs in a heat wave) mis-prices every city's high market the same way — bounded
  only by the 20% allocation cap. And entries are mostly NO bought at a median 0.56 (cap 0.70), an unfavorable
  payoff (avg win +$145 vs avg loss −$201). **Fix:** per-metric sub-cap (all-highs / all-lows each ≤~10–12%); lower
  max entry price to ~0.55–0.60; cut `KELLY_FRACTION` to ~0.02 or go flat-tiny until an edge is proven.

## 6. Execution & live-readiness (findings #9, #12, #13, #17, #18, #22)

- **#9 The day-ahead maker thesis is failing in the sim.** working_orders: intended cash $5,715 → filled $212
  (**3.7%**); **0 FILLED, 25 EXPIRED.** Orders rest 6h, posted 1–2 days *before* the target, so they expire the night
  *before* the extreme forms — they're resting in the wrong window. The maker edge is **entirely unproven** (11 maker
  trades, all still unsettled). **Fix:** rest closer to the target day with a settlement-aware GTD, or re-post across
  the day; **treat realized fill-rate as the kill-switch it was meant to be — it's currently failing.**
- **#12 "Go live by flipping `SIMULATION_MODE`" is false.** `LivePolymarketExecutor` is never instantiated; both
  maker paths hardcode the simulated executor and the **taker writes a Trade row with no executor at all** (no live
  taker path exists). Going live is a *build*: pin `py-clob-client`, add an executor factory wired through both
  paths, build live taker submission (FAK/FOK), wire the Polymarket key/funder, add reconciliation. **The maker fill
  simulation is also optimistic** (credits fills from any print through the limit regardless of aggressor side; queue
  snapshot never grows) — never use sim fills for the go-live gate, only real fills.
- **#13 Security blocker before any non-local deploy.** All control endpoints are unauthenticated behind
  `CORS allow_origins=['*']`; `/api/bot/reset` deletes all trades and resets bankroll. **Fix:** API-key/bearer on
  every mutating endpoint + restrict CORS, or bind localhost + SSH-tunnel only.
- **#17 Taker/maker double-book [happened ✅].** Tokyo 26°C 2026-06-29 has **two** open NO positions (a taker trade
  and a maker fill) because the taker's dedup checks only existing Trades, not resting WorkingOrders. **One-line
  fix:** `scheduler.py:180` should call the existing `maker._already_active` (which checks both).
- **#18 Settlement clock is UTC, not station-local** → settles up to 6h early (US) / 8h late (Asia). No misgrade yet
  (the decisive-price guard protected it), but it's latent risk for LOW markets. **Fix:** use `station_local_now`.
- **#22 Latent fee unit bug** in the taker (adds the fee fraction as an absolute price). Harmless at fee=0; fix
  before enabling any non-zero fee (e.g. Kalshi).

## 7. Storage, reproducibility & results integrity (findings #14, #15, #23, #24)

- **#14 You cannot reproduce or re-grade a trade.** The only record of the forecast that drove a trade is the
  free-text `signal.reasoning` string — there are no structured columns for ensemble mean, σ_eff, member count,
  bias, pricing center, obs-so-far, lead days, or the consumed order-book levels. This **blocks diagnosing why the
  model loses.** **Fix:** persist a structured point-in-time snapshot (JSON blob on Signal) at scan time. This is a
  prerequisite for the P&L backtest *and* for any honest post-mortem.
- **#15 Two ledgers, one headline.** `bot_state` (what the bot *sizes off*) says +$211/$10,211; `/api/stats` (the
  go/no-go headline) says −$924/$9,076 — different scopes (all-cities vs active) presented as "the" number, and
  `/api/stats` even mixes them (active-only bankroll but all-cities-scale caps). **Fix:** show both ledgers
  explicitly labelled; compute caps/percentages off the displayed bankroll; align breaker and dashboard scope.
- **#23/#24** Trade→signal linkage is a fragile "latest unexecuted signal for this ticker" heuristic (already
  mislinks 1 trade; 1 FILTERED signal mis-tagged executed) → pass the real signal id through. Orphaned legacy tables
  (`ai_logs`, `scan_logs`, `btc_price_snapshots`) still exist despite "removed" docs → drop them.

## 9. Roadmap to profitability (prioritized)

   excludes it by >~1.5σ; tighten the market-gap guardrail to a fraction of bucket width (#1, #7). *Done = the count
   of NO-on-modal trades drops to ~0 in a dry-run scan; live actionable list no longer shows 25%+ "edges."*
2. **Add a trailing-drawdown halt + tighten/forward-date the daily breaker** (#10, #11). *Done = a simulated −10%
   peak-to-trough pauses new entries; daily breaker includes unrealized MTM.*
3. **De-risk sizing while the edge is unproven:** `KELLY_FRACTION`→0.02 (or flat tiny), max entry price→0.55–0.60
   (#8, #21). *Done = max bet ≤ ~1% bankroll; no entries above 0.60.*
4. **Fix the double-book (#17) and the settlement clock (#18)** — both ~one-line. *Done = `_already_active` used in
   taker dedup; settlement uses station-local date; no duplicate open positions.*
5. **Lock down the control endpoints (#13)** before the bot is reachable off localhost. *Done = mutating endpoints
   require auth; CORS restricted.*
6. **Fix the dual-ledger dashboard (#15)** so the headline number is unambiguous. *Done = active scorecard AND true
   account both shown, caps computed off the displayed bankroll.*
7. **Re-score the calibration backtest at the true live width and fix the Asia proxy time** (#3) — change two
   constants. *Done = the harness reports model Brier at ~5°F (and a lead-aligned market snapshot); the "parity"
   line disappears and matches the live scoreboard.*

### Bigger builds — the actual path to an edge (in order)
1. **Per-city day-ahead width calibration** to replace the single global σ-inflation (#6). Fit width per
   (city, metric, lead) from *true day-ahead* forecast-vs-settlement residuals. *Done = Chicago/inland width ≈1.5–3°F
   restored; model Brier ≤ market on at least the inland slice at the deployed width, with CI.*
2. **A probability-calibration layer** (isotonic/Platt on the reliability curve) feeding the gate and Kelly (#2,
   #16). *Done = stated conviction ≈ realized frequency (overconfidence gap < ~0.05) on held-out data.*
3. **A real event-driven P&L backtest of the actual rules** (§3d). *Done = an OOS, cost-inclusive P&L curve for the
   inland slice with a hit-rate and drawdown; this is the gate for any scaling.*
4. **Lead-correct, point-in-time storage** (#14) — prerequisite for #3 and for diagnosing live trades.
5. **Cut Tokyo/HK from active trading; keep only slices with a proven center** (Chicago first, inland next), and
   **rolling/seasonal bias re-fit keyed to each market's settlement provider** (#20). *Done = active set = cities with
   CI-positive center skill only.*
6. **Drive forecast RMSE down toward the market** (MOS / station downscaling to cut representativeness error) — the
   only way to earn a *general* day-ahead edge rather than a narrow inland one.
7. **Redefine "edge" as a pre-registered shape-based mechanism** and add a standing monotonicity monitor
   (corr of edge vs realized win / Brier-gap) to the scoreboard (#5). *Done = the bot only trades where edge is
   measured to be positively selective.*
8. **Build the live execution path** (#12) — *last*, only after #3 proves an edge.

### The decision gate
**Do not scale capital until the event-driven P&L backtest shows the inland slice beats the price net of the 8% gate
and spread, out-of-sample.** Judge on **Brier per slice with a bootstrap CI excluding zero**, not on P&L (which is
too noisy at this trade count). Until then this is an R&D system, not an income system — which is the correct and
honest place for it to be.
